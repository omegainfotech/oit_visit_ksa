KINGDOM OF SAUDIA ARABIA

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, commonly referred to as Saudi Arabia, is a Middle Eastern country located on the Arabian Peninsula. Here are some main points about Saudi Arabia in theory: Geography: Saudi Arabia is the largest country on the Arabian Peninsula, covering an area of approximately 2.15 million square kilometers. It is characterized by diverse landscapes, including deserts, mountains, and coastlines along the Red Sea and the Arabian Gulf. Government: Saudi Arabia is an absolute monarchy with a hereditary monarchy system. The King is both the head of state and the head of government, holding vast powers and authority. The country follows Islamic law (Sharia) as the basis for its legal system. Religion: Saudi Arabia is the birthplace of Islam and is home to its two holiest cities, Mecca and Medina. Islam is the state religion, and the country adheres to a conservative interpretation of Sunni Islam known as Wahhabism. Economy: Saudi Arabia has one of the world's largest oil reserves and is a major oil producer and exporter. The economy heavily relies on the oil sector, which has historically been the main driver of its wealth. Efforts have been made to diversify the economy through the Saudi Vision 2030 plan, which aims to reduce dependence on oil and promote other sectors such as tourism, entertainment, and technology.
